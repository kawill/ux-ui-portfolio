Overview

What is TableShare?

TableShare was designed as a potential solution for making a café a more productive and collaborative environment for adult students and workers. It builds upon the findings that adults studying and working at a café typically work alone but are willing to share their workspace with other focused students and workers.

TableShare allows users to get to work faster by providing relevant information about how many tables are available to work at and who a user can potentially sit with. The app may minimize the chance a user and customer must leave the café for lack of seating. The app may also minimize interruptions by others asking to share a table. As a result, a café owner may provide a more consistent and predictable working environment.

Goal

Keep working and studying customers comfortable and productive in a full café.

Role

  • Conducted and synthesized user research
  • Developed user flows, sketches, wireframes and high-fidelity, interactive prototypes
  • Conducted usability testing

Summary

For this project, I followed the Double Diamond process of discovery, synthesis, and several rounds of design and refinement. After deducing and synthesizing insights from several interviews, I found an opportunity to create an app that allows users to find tables to share in crowded cafes. Several usability tests brought out significant pain points to address. These results allowed me to make significant improvements in the user flow and design in the following iterations.

Discovery

Research & Analysis

I initially conducted interviews with the intent to understand adults’, who are currently studying, view of what makes for a productive learning environment, especially in a café.

Affinity mapping of the interview data resulted in the understanding that adult learners were primarily interested in studying or working alone but around similarly-minded/focused adults.

An additional set of interviews conducted by the instructor and analyzed by me through affinity mapping, revealed the opportunity to provide a more consistent and predictable working environment for those working in the café.

Synthesis

Themes & Opportunities

Six themes and opportunities emerged from my research analysis that I synthesized into a single question: how might I get customers to their work or study faster in a café.

Design & Refine

Ideation

In order to answer the question of how to get customers to work faster in the café, I ideated 11 possible features of a digital solution.

In order to produce a solution quickly, I would need to focus on a few features. I choose 3 features to focus on based on the interview data and a Value-Cost/Complexity analysis.

Sketching

I explored each feature further through the brainstorming sketching method, crazy 8’s.

Prototyping — Lo–Fi Iteration 1

Since my goals were to: 1) eliminate the discomfort associated with asking to share a table at a crowded café 2) lower the amount of time spent looking for a table 3) lower the chances of a potential customer leaving:

I decided to focus first on rapidly prototyping the ability to select a table with at least one other person who is already seated and willing to share their table at a crowded café.

Remote, Moderated Usability Test & Feedback

A remote, moderated usability test revealed pain points in the user flow for all screens.

The prototype missed the mark of communicating who the app was for, the options the user had and confirmation of the selected the table.

Consequently, I iterated over the initial flow to add an additional page and button to help the user.

Prototyping — Lo–Fi Iteration 2

Iteration of the user flow led to the iteration of the low–fidelity prototype.

The second iteration of the Lo–Fi prototype:

  • Simplified the navigation
  • Presented the user with clearer options of table type
  • Added text to help the user understand there are others at the table
  • Added clearer status messages
  • Utilized fading/disabling and secondary buttons to further clarify the user’s options

Prototyping — Hi–Fi Iteration 1

I began to increase the fidelity of the second iteration of the Lo–Fi prototype in preparation for more usability testing.

Prototyping — Hi–Fi Iteration 2

I assessed the accessibility of the first Hi–Fi iteration and updated accordingly.

Majority of the changes involved:

  • Updating the color contrasts to have a WCAG AAA rating
  • Creating a secondary and tertiary button
  • Using symbols/icons for clarity
  • Updated messaging

Retrospective

What went well?

  • Using UI Kits for wireframing and iPhone iOS components allowed for faster and, eventually, more realistic prototyping.
  • Usability testing brought out significant pain points to address, allowing me to make significant improvements in the iterations directly after testing.

Areas for Improvement

  • It was sometimes difficult to understand how much to prime usability test participants, I had difficulty determining how much to allow participants to know.
  • Finding participants to test prototypes was difficult.

Constraints & Considerations

TableShare essentially turns each café table into a potential coworking space. However, this is not permanent. A table’s status is dynamic and may change according to who is seated there. In order to be implemented, TableShare may require at least a part of the café devoted to app users.

Next Steps

  • Add more testing throughout development
  • Determine more places to quickly recruit participants
  • Explore Onboarding of TableShare
